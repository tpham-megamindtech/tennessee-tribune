Franklin officials confirmed the update this week, saying it reflects months of planning and public input from residents across the area.

"This is something our community has been asking for, and we're glad to finally see it come together," said Jennifer Martin, county commissioner in Franklin.

Planning documents reviewed by the Tennessee Tribune show the project is expected to cost roughly $8.6 million, with work phased in over the next 1 to 2 years. Local leaders say the timeline could shift depending on funding and weather.

> "We're proud of what this means for Franklin and the people who live here." — Jennifer Martin

Residents can find updates and opportunities to comment through the city's official channels in the weeks ahead.
