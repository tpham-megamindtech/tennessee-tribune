The news comes as beauty businesses across Knoxville report steady demand from both longtime regulars and a growing number of first-time clients.

"We've put a lot of work into getting this right, and we think people are going to notice the difference," said Kayla Thomas, beauty educator in Knoxville.

Staff say appointment requests have climbed roughly 32 percent since word first spread on social media. The business currently employs 7 licensed technicians and plans to add more positions if demand keeps pace.

> "This is exactly the kind of progress our community deserves." — Kayla Thomas

Those interested can book online or by phone, and walk-ins are welcome as availability allows.
