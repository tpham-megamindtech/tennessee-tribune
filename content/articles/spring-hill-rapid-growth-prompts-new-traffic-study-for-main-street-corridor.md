"We've put a lot of work into getting this right, and we think people are going to notice the difference," said Tyler Moore, school board chair in Spring Hill.

Planning documents reviewed by the Tennessee Tribune show the project is expected to cost roughly $5.9 million, with work phased in over the next 2 to 3 years. Local leaders say the timeline could shift depending on funding and weather.
