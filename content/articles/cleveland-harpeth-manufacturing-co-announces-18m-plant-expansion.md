The announcement adds to a string of recent developments for the Cleveland business community, which has seen steady growth over the past year.

"We've put a lot of work into getting this right, and we think people are going to notice the difference," said David Brown, small business owner in Cleveland.

Company leaders point to Cleveland's growing workforce and lower cost of doing business compared with larger metro markets. The move is expected to create or support roughly 75 jobs in the region over the next two years.

> "This is exactly the kind of progress our community deserves." — David Brown

Local economic development officials say they will continue tracking the impact on jobs and tax revenue in the months ahead.
