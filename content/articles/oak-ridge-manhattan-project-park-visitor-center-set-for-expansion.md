Oak Ridge officials confirmed the update this week, saying it reflects months of planning and public input from residents across the area.

"We've put a lot of work into getting this right, and we think people are going to notice the difference," said Nicole Davis, county commissioner in Oak Ridge.

Planning documents reviewed by the Tennessee Tribune show the project is expected to cost roughly $2.6 million, with work phased in over the next 2 to 3 years. Local leaders say the timeline could shift depending on funding and weather.

> "This is exactly the kind of progress our community deserves." — Nicole Davis

Residents can find updates and opportunities to comment through the city's official channels in the weeks ahead.
