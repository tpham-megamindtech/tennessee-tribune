Maryville officials confirmed the update this week, saying it reflects months of planning and public input from residents across the area.

"It's a big step forward for Maryville, and it wouldn't have happened without a lot of people pulling in the same direction," said Andrew Miller, deputy mayor.

Planning documents reviewed by the Tennessee Tribune show the project is expected to cost roughly $3.7 million, with work phased in over the next 3 to 4 years. Local leaders say the timeline could shift depending on funding and weather.

> "It took teamwork, but we got there." — Andrew Miller

Residents can find updates and opportunities to comment through the city's official channels in the weeks ahead.
