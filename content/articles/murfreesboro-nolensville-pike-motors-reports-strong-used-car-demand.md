The announcement adds to a string of recent developments for the Murfreesboro business community, which has seen steady growth over the past year.

"This is something our community has been asking for, and we're glad to finally see it come together," said Jason Harris, chamber of commerce director in Murfreesboro.

Company leaders point to Murfreesboro's growing workforce and lower cost of doing business compared with larger metro markets. The move is expected to create or support roughly 65 jobs in the region over the next two years.

> "We're proud of what this means for Murfreesboro and the people who live here." — Jason Harris

Local economic development officials say they will continue tracking the impact on jobs and tax revenue in the months ahead.
